Hypothesis: Population growth will slow as countries develop economically.

Comment on choice of GDP per capita:

Gdp/capita, median income/capita, gini-adjusted gdp/capita, none of these are very good to indicate prosperity, generally, since they don't take into account the distribution. Even median income is flawed, since growth at median doesn't imply growth at bottom

While the hypothesis seems true up until 1975, the relationship doesn't hold after that. Perhaps the use of GDP/Capita may partially account for this uptick in fertility as income increased between 1976 and 2010. While GDP per capita seems to have increased, perhaps it actually decreased due to concentration of wealth in the top percentiles. It is just as likely though that there is a division where this relationship no longer holds, when fertility gets to be around the "replacement rate" (about 2 births per female).

The relationship is not so clear when looking at Africa possibly because the ranges of income and fertility rates are quite small.
